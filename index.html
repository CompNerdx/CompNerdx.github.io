The History of the Internet

The internet has been one of the most influential innovations of our time. It has done so much for the world. We have done research on the history of the internet and we are now sharing it with you. We picked three questions to research, and they are below. You can read about what we found by clicking on them.

Why was the internet invented?

When Sputnik was launched, America went into a bit of a panic. They realized that the USSR had readily advancing technology, and phone lines would be pretty bad for a communication system because they could be easily cut. They decided that they needed something that would distribute the information and rerout it in case of a breakage, so packet switching was created.

In addition to this, there weren't many high power computers at the time, and people that needed to use them were far away from them. They realized that by linking computers together, people with low power terminals could access high power machines from across the country.

This is the basis of which the ARPAnet was built on, which was one of the first computer networks, and was ultimately vital in the creation of the internet we know today.

Who invented the Internet? How was it invented?

There is no single person that created the internet by themselves. There were many people that helped in the creation of the internet that contributed many different things. For example, one of the key inventions that formed the internet was packet switching.

Packet switching was first theorized by Paul Baran and Donald Davies in the early 60's, and later Leonard Kleinrock did further research on it. He went on to implement the first packet switching network, the ARPAnet, in the late 60s. This formed the basis of the internet.

Another important innovation was the TCP/IP protocol, developed by Robert Khan and Vinton Cerf, which set standards for networks communitcating with other networks, which was huge in the development of the internet and is a very important piece of the Internet today.

Possibly the most well known of all the innovations and people that made the internet what it is today is the World Wide Web and the HTTP protocol, invented by Tim Berners-Lee. This is a hypertext protocol that allows hypertext to be displayed over the internet in websites, and is probably the most widely used piece of the modern internet.
